Sibu is an inland town at the central region of Sarawak and the capital of Sibu District in Sibu Division, Sarawak, Malaysia.

The town is located on the island of Borneo and covers an area of 129.5 square kilometres (50.0 sq mi).

It is located at the confluence of the Rajang and Igan Rivers, some 60 kilometres from the South China Seaand approximately 191.5 kilometres (119 mi) north-east of the state capital Kuching.

Sibu is mainly populated by people of Chinese descent, mainly from Fuzhou. Other ethnic groups such as Melanau, Malay, and Iban are also present, but unlike other regions of Sarawak, they are not as significant.
